Lil Boosie Childhood

Lil Boosie grew up in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, where he was raised by his parents, Raymond and Jacquelyn Hatch, in a low-income family. His mother, who was a teacher, influenced him to become interested in poetry at a young age. He also developed a passion for basketball, so he worked on his game while attending McKinley High School. He actually dropped out of school but later earned his GED while serving a prison sentence. Following his father’s death from a drug overdose in 1996, Lil Boosie shifted his focus from poetry to songwriting and decided to pursue a career in the music industry.

Lil Boosie Career, Professional Life

Lil Boosie’s professional career began in 1996 when he joined Concentration Camp, a hip hop collective comprised of Boo, C-Loc, Happy Perez, J-Von, Lucky Knuckles, Max Minelli, and Young Bleed; he was the group’s youngest member at the time, being only 14 years old. He made his debut on C-fifth Loc’s album, “It’s A Gamble,” and then appeared on the group’s third album, “Camp III: Thug Brothas,” both in 2000. Following his departure from the Camp, Young Bleed distinguished himself sufficiently to move to a prime position as a rapper alongside C-Loc and Max Minelli, which encouraged him to pursue his career further and more seriously.

In August 2000, Lil Boosie, then 17, released his debut solo studio album, “Youngest Of Da Camp.” The following year, he signed a deal with Pimp C’s record label Trill Entertainment and released his second studio album, “For My Thugz,” featuring Webbie, Pimp C, and Young Bleed, followed by his debut mixtape, “Boosie 2002 (Advance),” which significantly increased his net worth. During the next two years, he also collaborated with Webbie on two albums: “Ghetto Stories” (2003) and “Gangsta Musik” (2004), which featured the hit song “Give Me That.”

More On Career

Lil Boosie continued to build on his success by releasing a number of mixtapes, including “Da Beginning,” “Thug Passion,” and “Gone Til December,” among others, before releasing his next studio album, “Superbad: The Return Of Boosie Bad Azz,” in September 2009. The album was released by his own record label, Bad Azz Entertainment, and debuted at No. 7 on the US Billboard 200 chart with the lead single “Better Believe It,” featuring Webbie and Young Jeezy. By the end of the decade, he had released another studio album, “Incarcerated,” which peaked at No. 13 on the US Billboard 200, No. 6 on the US Billboard US Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums, and No. 4 on the US Billboard Top Rap Albums, adding significantly to his net worth.
