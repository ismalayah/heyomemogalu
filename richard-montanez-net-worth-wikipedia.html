Richard Montanez Net Worth 2023, Age, Wikipedia, Wife

Richard Montanez is an author and businessman who is well known for claiming to have invented Flamin’ Hot Cheetos. According to him, he and his wife, Judy, invented the spicy sensation right in their own kitchen, back when Richard was a factory worker at a Frito-Lay plant in Southern California.

Richard Montanez, who started his career at Frito-Lay as a factory janitor and subsequently became the company’s marketing director, claimed in the late 2000s that he created Flamin’ Hot Cheetos in the early 1990s. He also stated that he had submitted his idea to CEO Roger Enrico.

According to the Los Angeles Times in May 2021, Frito-Lay began an internal inquiry in 2018 when Greenfeld filed a complaint. The company said of the results of that investigation:

None of our records show that Richard [Montañez] was involved in any capacity in the Flamin’ Hot test market … we do not credit the creation of Flamin’ Hot Cheetos or any Flamin’ Hot products to him.

However, according to a 1993 feature in U.S. News & World Report, Richard was responsible for recommending product ideas that built on the Flamin’ Hotline, including Flamin’ Hot Popcorn. Following the LA Times inquiry, he has stood by his claims, arguing that there was a lack of paperwork due to his low-level work at the time.

Richard Montanez Wiki, Biography

Richard Montanez was born on 15 August 1958 and was raised in a California migrant labor camp. Sadly, he hasn’t said much about his parents or family. However, we do know that his parents are Mexican immigrants. He was brought up alongside his ten siblings. Richard dropped out of school and worked as a laborer until getting employed as a janitor for Frito-Lay, at its Rancho Cucamonga factory, in 1976 when he was 18 years old.

He is most known for creating the Flaming hotline of goods, which included Flaming Hot Cheetos. In addition, Richard influenced KFC and Taco Bell’s Hispanic goods and marketing initiatives. For your information, he was named one of the most important Hispanic’s in corporate America by Hispanic Lifestyle magazine, and he has been featured in News Week and Fortune 500 magazine.
